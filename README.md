# Housefly Mediated Antibiotic Resistance Analysis

## Overview
This project explores how houseflies can carry antibiotic resistant bacteria across different urban environments.

Samples were collected from 7 areas and 3 different zone types:
- Residential area
- Street food area
- Fish market

A total of 52 houseflies were collected. From these, 363 bacterial colonies were isolated. After PCR confirmation, 140 bacterial isolates were analyzed.

Antibiotic susceptibility testing (AST) was performed on all confirmed isolates.


## Key Findings

-- Antibiotic resistance was very high in several commonly used antibiotics.

-- Ciprofloxacin and Meropenem showed higher sensitivity compared to others. These antibiotics are still relatively effective.

-- Resistance varied across organisms. Pseudomonas aeruginosa and Vibrio spp. showed higher resistance patterns compared to others.

-- Area-based variation was observed. Some locations showed consistently higher resistance levels, indicating possible environmental or human activity influence.

-- Zone type comparison showed that street food and fish market areas had slightly higher resistance than residential areas.

-- Seasonal variation was also observed. Resistance levels were higher in summer compared to winter, suggesting environmental factors may influence bacterial survival and resistance.

-- Growth patterns based on dilution levels showed that some organisms remained viable even at higher dilution levels, indicating strong adaptability.

## Interpretation

-- The results suggest that houseflies can act as mechanical vectors of antibiotic resistant bacteria.

-- Their movement between waste, food, and human environments increases the risk of spreading resistant pathogens.

-- This is especially concerning for public health in densely populated urban areas.
